PHASE: critical | This week — urgent | Start today

SECTION: Consulting case prep

ITEM: tag=product | effort=1 hr/day | Complete one full case per day
Use Case in Point or prepLounge. Time yourself strictly — 40 minutes per case. Write down what you got wrong after each one.

ITEM: tag=product | effort=2 hrs | Map your weak areas
After 5 cases, identify the frameworks you keep missing. Profitability? Market sizing? M&A? Focus the next week on those only.

ITEM: tag=content | effort=1 hr | Research your target firms' case styles
McKinsey, BCG, and Bain all have different formats. Know which you're targeting and find 3 example cases from each.

ITEM: tag=product | effort=30 min | Find a case partner
prepLounge or your university network. Aim for 3 sessions per week — verbal practice is different from solo prep.

SECTION: Degree decision — GMAT vs CEMS vs double degree

ITEM: tag=product | effort=2 hrs | List your actual criteria
Write down what you want in 3 years. Then score each option (GMAT/MBA, CEMS, double degree) against those criteria honestly. Not what sounds impressive — what actually fits your goals.

ITEM: tag=product | effort=1 hr | Research CEMS deadlines
Find the exact application deadline, required GPA, partner schools available from your university, and language requirements.

ITEM: tag=product | effort=1 hr | Research double degree options
Which partner universities does your school offer? What are the application windows? Do any overlap with consulting recruiting timelines?

ITEM: tag=product | effort=1 hr | Speak to someone doing each path
One person doing CEMS, one doing a double degree, one who took the GMAT route. 20-minute calls. Ask what they wish they knew before deciding.

ITEM: tag=content | effort=30 min | Set a hard decision date
Pick a date by which you will have decided. Put it in your calendar. Decisions made under open-ended timelines never get made.

PHASE: soon | Business idea — this week | Evaluate and pick one

SECTION: Finding a winning idea

ITEM: tag=growth | effort=2 hrs | Generate 10 ideas using the right filter
A good idea for you right now: something you can build with AI in days, that solves a real pain for a specific person, that you can start marketing next week without a finished product. Write 10. Don't evaluate yet.

ITEM: tag=growth | effort=1 hr | Score each idea on 4 criteria
(1) Can you build an MVP with AI in under a week? (2) Do you know exactly who the customer is? (3) Can you reach them on LinkedIn or Instagram? (4) Would you pay for this yourself? Score 1-3 on each.

ITEM: tag=growth | effort=30 min | Pick the top 2 and stress-test them
For each: who is the first 10 customers and how do you reach them? If you can't name them specifically, the idea is too vague.

ITEM: tag=growth | effort=30 min | Make the final call
Pick one. The goal is not to find the perfect idea — it is to pick something specific enough to act on. You can pivot. You cannot build nothing.

SECTION: Build the MVP with AI

ITEM: tag=product | effort=3 hrs | Define what the MVP does in one sentence
Not a paragraph. One sentence. If you cannot describe it in one sentence it is not specific enough yet.

ITEM: tag=product | effort=1 day | Build V1 using Claude or Cursor
Use Claude Code or Cursor to build the simplest possible version. It does not need to be polished. It needs to do the one thing.

ITEM: tag=product | effort=1 hr | Get 3 people to try it before you market it
Not for feedback on features — for one question: did it solve the problem you promised? If yes, start marketing. If no, fix that one thing first.

PHASE: soon | Marketing — start next week | Before the product is perfect

SECTION: First marketing actions

ITEM: tag=growth | effort=1 hr | Write your first LinkedIn post
Not a product announcement. A story: the problem you noticed, why it matters, what you built. Specific and personal beats polished and generic every time.

ITEM: tag=growth | effort=30 min | DM 10 people who fit your target customer
Not a pitch. One sentence describing the problem and asking if they experience it. If 3 out of 10 say yes, you have something worth building.

ITEM: tag=content | effort=1 hr | Write 5 posts for next week scheduled
One per day. Rotate: problem post, solution post, behind-the-scenes post, customer insight post, personal story post. Write them all now so you do not have to think about it daily.

ITEM: tag=growth | effort=30 min | Set up a simple landing page
One headline, one subheadline, one email capture. Carrd.co is free and takes 20 minutes. You need somewhere to send people.
